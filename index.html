Course Description

AI is transforming how information is created, curated, and consumed. Through a semester-long Design-Based Research (DBR) cycle, you will investigate authentic P-12 challenges and design playful learning experiences that build AI literacy.

AI as Subject

What students must understand about technology mechanics.

AI as Tool

How generative tools support instruction and creative workflows.

AI as Ethics

Addressing bias, privacy, and environmental impact.

Learning Objectives

  • Analyze AI transformation of P-12 information ecosystems.
  • Evaluate AI-generated content for bias and reliability.
  • Identify ethical issues surrounding AI in schools.
  • Apply literacy frameworks to instructional design.
  • Design developmentally appropriate P-12 instruction.
  • Conduct iterative prototyping using DBR methodology.

P-12 Grade Band Differentiation

Elementary (K-5)

Encounters: YouTube Kids, Voice Assistants, algorithms.

"Recognizing AI in daily life; questioning habits; Robot or Human guessing games."

Middle (6-8)

Encounters: Snapchat My AI, TikTok, Canva, ChatGPT.

"Filter bubble investigations; prompt literacy; understanding recommendation systems."

High (9-12)

Encounters: Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, image/video generators.

"Deepfake literacy; algorithm analysis; copyright debates; policy design."
